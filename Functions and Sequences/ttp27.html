19. Functions and Sequences

19.23.1 Finding the sum of a set of consecutive terms in the sequence

When were asked to find the sum of a set of consecutive terms in a sequence, we will first determine whether the sequence repeats. If the sequence is a repeating sequence, then we will follow the steps we took in the earlier lesson to find the required sum. If the sequence is not repeating, it is likely that many of the terms will cancel out.

Must Know:
Toggle bookmark

When asked to find the sum of several consecutive terms in a sequence, first determine whether the sequence is a repeating sequence. If it is a repeating sequence, apply the strategies you learned in the Repeating Sequences lesson. If the sequence is not a repeating sequence, list a few terms at the beginning and end of the sequence to gain a better understanding of the required sum.

Consider this mini-example:

In the sequence a1, a2, a3, . . . an, . . . , the nth term is defined as an=1n1n+1 for all integers n 1.

What is the sum of the first 50 terms in the sequence?

Since it would take prohibitively long to add the first 50 terms of the sequence, its reasonable to suspect that many of the values in the sum will cancel. To confirm this, we can list a few terms at the beginning and end of the sequence to gain a better understanding of the required sum.

Lets use the formula an=1n1n+1 to determine the first few terms of the sequence:

an=1n1n+1a1=1111+1=1112a2=1212+1=1213a3=1313+1=1314a4=1414+1=1415a5=1515+1=1516

So, the first five terms of the sequence are (1112),(1213),(1314),(1415),and(1516).

We can now see that the sequence is not a repeating sequence.

Notice also that we didnt bother performing the subtractions to find the final terms in the sequence. If we wished, we could have subtracted the above fractions to find that the first five terms of the sequence are 12,16,112,120,and130. However, if we simplify each term, we make it much harder to see which values will cancel out, which makes it harder to calculate the required sum.

Must Know:

When calculating the terms needed for a sum in which you suspect values might cancel, do not immediately perform every subtraction, since it may be easier to see which values cancel out if the terms still contain those subtractions. Later, if necessary, you can simplify each term.

Coordinate Geometry is Currently Tested on the GMAT
Updated on: Jul 2025

While most geometrical concepts were removed from the GMAT Focus Edition, which GMAC now refers to simply as "the GMAT" once again, Coordinate Geometry is still tested.

In addition, some basic geometry concepts, including calculating the area and perimeter of shapes like squares, triangles, and circles, are still tested on the current GMAT. However, whenever a geometry formula outside of coordinate geometry is required, it will be provided in the question stem.
