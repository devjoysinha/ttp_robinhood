10. Work Problems

10.7.1 Two objects work together for the same amount of time

These problems are easy to recognize because the objects in the problem start a job together at the same time and end the job together at the same time. These are some of the most common types of work problems. Lets look at an example.

Text Solution:

This is a combined worker problem with 2 people. If Joe can paint the fence in 4 hours and his brother can paint the fence in 2 hours, then their rates are

RateJoe=WorkTime=1fence4hours=14fencehourRateBrother=WorkTime=1fence2hours=12fencehour

Given that Joe and his brother work together for the same amount of unknown time, we can represent both their times by the variable t:

Please note, in using this approach, 2 hours and 4 hours are not entered into the time cells, because those cells are reserved for the time that Joe and his brother spend working together. The time differential only refers to their respective work rates, not the time it takes to complete this particular task.  Also, we are told that they spend the same amount of time painting together, so using 4 hours and 2 hours for time would not make sense.

Knowing both the rate and the time for each, we can calculate the amount of work that each person contributes to the total task, in terms of t, when they paint the fence together:

We can now create an equation based on our understanding that the total work done is the sum of the individual work values. Joes work (t4fences) + his brothers work (t2fences) must add up to the total work done between the two of them (1 fence). We can write the following equation:

WorkJoe+WorkBrother=WorkTotalt4+t2=14(t4+t2=1)t+2t=4t=43=113hours

Because 13 of an hour is 20 minutes, the total time to complete the job together is 1 hour and 20 minutes.

Example 9 DI Data Sufficiency

easy
Once solved, create a new example with AI using this one as a starting point, tailored to the difficulty level you choose.
Need a Little Help?
Hi Joy, do you need a hint for this example?

How long will it take machine A, working alone at a constant rate, to produce 20 widgets?

1) When machine A works with machine B, they produce 40 widgets in 2 minutes while working at their respective rates.

2) Machine B, working alone at a constant rate, produces 20 widgets in 4 minutes.

Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) alone is not sufficient.
Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) alone is not sufficient.
BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.
Confirm your answer
Show solution Hide solution
PRESENTED BY :
Scott Woodbury-Stewart Founder and Expert GMAT Instructor Scott Woodbury-Stewart Founder and Expert GMAT Instructor

Text Solution:

Question Stem Analysis:

We need to determine how long it will take machine A to produce 20 widgets. To calculate this, we need to determine machine As work rate.

Statement One Alone:

 When machine A works with machine B, they produce 40 widgets in 2 minutes while working at their respective rates.

 

Rate

Time

Work

Machine A

Awidgetsminute

2 minutes

2A widgets

Machine B

Bwidgetsminute

2 minutes

2B widgets

Machine As work plus machine Bs work must equal 40 widgets:

 2A + 2B = 40 widgets

We can see that we have too many variables to determine the rate of machine A. Statement one alone is not sufficient.

Eliminate answer choices A and D.

Statement Two Alone:

 Machine B, working alone at a constant rate, produces 20 widgets in 4 minutes.

If machine B produces 20 widgets in 4 minutes, its work rate is

RateMachineB=WorkTime=20 widgets4minutes=5widgetsminute

However, we are not given any information about machine A. Statement two alone is not sufficient.

Eliminate answer choice B.

Statements One and Two Together:

From statement one, we let the rate of machine A be A and the rate of machine B be B, producing the equation: (2A + 2B) = 40. From statement two, we determined that machine Bs rate is 5widgetsminute. We can use these two pieces of information to produce the following equation:

2A+2(5)=402A=30A=15widgetsminute

Thus, machine As rate is 15widgetsminute, and its time to produce 20 widgets would be

Time=WorkRate=20widgets15widgetsminute=43minutes

Therefore, statements one and two together are sufficient.

Correct answer: C

Coordinate Geometry is Currently Tested on the GMAT
Updated on: Jul 2025

While most geometrical concepts were removed from the GMAT Focus Edition, which GMAC now refers to simply as "the GMAT" once again, Coordinate Geometry is still tested.

In addition, some basic geometry concepts, including calculating the area and perimeter of shapes like squares, triangles, and circles, are still tested on the current GMAT. However, whenever a geometry formula outside of coordinate geometry is required, it will be provided in the question stem.
