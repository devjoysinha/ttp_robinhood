At Least Probability Problems

The phrase “at least” means “equal to or greater than.”
For example: “What is the probability the coin shows heads at least two times in three flips?” That means heads comes up two or three times.

General Process

  1. Define the scenarios. List all ways that qualify as “at least n.”
  2. Calculate the probability of each scenario.
  3. Add those probabilities. The final probability is the sum of the mutually exclusive scenario probabilities.
Must Know: Solve “at least” problems by splitting into all scenarios that qualify and adding their probabilities.

Example 1: Coin Flips

A fair coin is tossed 3 times. What is the probability that it lands on heads at least 2 times?

Show Solution Step 1: Define scenarios
  • Heads exactly 2 times: HHT, HTH, THH (in any order)
  • Heads 3 times: HHH
Step 2: Find probability for each scenario
  • Each outcome has probability (1/2)3 = 1/8.
  • Exactly 2 heads: there are C(3,2) = 3 ways.
    Probability = 3 × (1/8) = 3/8
  • Exactly 3 heads: 1 way, probability = 1/8
Step 3: Add them up:
Final probability = 3/8 + 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2

Example 2: Biased Probability

In City Y, the probability of more than 5 inches of snow in a storm is 1/3. If there are 3 storms, what is the probability it snows more than 5 inches in at least 2 of them?

  • A. 1/27
  • B. 3/27
  • C. 5/64
  • D. 7/27
  • E. 10/27
Show Solution Let 'Y' = snow >5", 'N' = not >5".
  • P(Y) = 1/3, P(N) = 2/3
  • At least 2 means: (a) Exactly 2 storms >5", or (b) All 3 storms >5".
(a) Exactly 2 'Y', 1 'N':
  • # ways: C(3,2) = 3
  • Each way: (1/3)×(1/3)×(2/3) = (1×1×2)/(3×3×3) = 2/27
  • Sum for all arrangements: 3 × (2/27) = 6/27
(b) All 3 'Y':
  • Only one way: (1/3)3 = 1/27
Total = 6/27 + 1/27 = 7/27
Final Answer: 7/27 (Choice D)

Example 3: At least 5 tails in 6 fair coin tosses

A fair coin is flipped 6 times. What is the probability that the coin lands on tails at least 5 times?

  • A. 7/64
  • B. 6/64
  • C. 5/64
  • D. 3/64
  • E. 1/64
Show Solution
  • At least 5 tails means: exactly 5 tails or all 6 tails
  • Exactly 5 tails, 1 head:
    Number of ways: C(6,1) = 6
    Probability for each: (1/2)6 = 1/64
    So: 6 × (1/64) = 6/64
  • All 6 tails: Only 1 way; probability = 1/64
Total = 6/64 + 1/64 = 7/64
Final Answer: 7/64 (Choice A)
