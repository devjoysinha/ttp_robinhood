Combinations with Restrictions: Some Items Must Be Chosen

In some combination problems, certain items must be selected. In this lesson, we review a general process to solve these problems.

Recall: In any combination problem, we select items from a “main pool” into a “subgroup.” When certain items must be chosen:

  • Visualize these must-select items as already placed in the subgroup.
  • Determine how many remain in the main pool, and how many spots remain to be filled in the subgroup.

Example:
If there is a pool of 20 people from which a 6-person committee is being formed, and Hattie & Maggie must be on the committee, visualize them as already chosen. There are now 18 people for 4 remaining spots.

Another Example:

There are 10 pieces of fruit, 2 are apples.
We're forming a group of 5, where both apples must be included.

Visualize both apples as already chosen: only 8 fruit remain, and 3 spots remain.

Example 1

Hank and Willie are among 10 people available to work a night shift at Company X. If a 4-person shift is to be formed and both Hank and Willie must be on it, how many different 4-person teams can be formed?

  • A. 12
  • B. 28
  • C. 36
  • D. 40
  • E. 56
See Solution
  1. Since Hank and Willie are chosen, imagine them already on the team; there are 8 people left and 2 spots remaining.
  2. The number of ways to choose 2 people from 8:
    C(8,2) = 8 × 7 ÷ 2 = 28 ways
Final Answer: 28

Example 2

Alex and Eric are 2 of 8 students trying out for a 5-person chess team. If Alex and Eric must be on the chess team, how many different chess teams are possible?

  • A. 12
  • B. 20
  • C. 42
  • D. 56
  • E. 64
See Solution
  1. Alex & Eric are already chosen. 6 students remain, and 3 spots to fill.
  2. Number of ways: C(6,3) = 6 × 5 × 4 / 3 × 2 = 120 / 6 = 20 ways
Final Answer: 20

Example 3

A treasure chest contains 5 different flawed diamonds and 2 different perfect diamonds.
How many distinct sets of 4 diamonds could be removed from the chest if the 2 perfect diamonds must be among the diamonds removed?

  • A. 10
  • B. 16
  • C. 18
  • D. 24
  • E. 32
See Solution
  • 2 perfect diamonds are already chosen, 5 flawed remain, 2 spots to fill.
  • C(5,2) = 5 × 4 ÷ 2 = 10 ways
Final Answer: 10
