7. General Word Problems

7.16 Objects in a Line

On test day, we may encounter questions that focus on a line of objects or people, like in the following examples:

Zara is waiting in line to get into a theater. If there are 10 people in front of Zara and 8 people behind her, how many people are in the line?

Claudio is waiting in line to enter an arena. If the total number of people in line is two less than four times the number of people in front of Claudio, and the number of people in front of Claudio is 23 less than the number of people behind Claudio, how many people are behind Claudio?

Two people, Adam and Ben, are waiting in a line to buy concert tickets. Adam is the 10th person counting from the front of the line, and Ben is the 15th person counting from the back of the line. If there are 5 people between the two men, how many people could be waiting in the line?

In this lesson, we will learn a systematic approach to answering such questions by focusing on the following two question types:

1 named object in the line

2 named objects in the line

Note: While its unlikely that you would ever encounter a question involving 3 or more named objects in a line, the strategies explained here can be expanded to handle such questions.

One named object in the line

Lets develop a technique for answering questions in which there is only one named object in the line by exploring the following mini-example:

Zara is waiting in line to get into a theater. If there are 10 people in front of Zara and 8 people behind her, how many people are in the line?

The steps for answering this question are as follows:

Step 1: On a line, add a point representing the named object in line and label the diagram such that x = the number of objects behind the object and y = the number of objects in front of the object.

In this case, the named object is Zara:

Important: The values of x and y do not include the named object, Zara. Thus, the total number of people in line is equal to the number of people behind Zara plus the number of people in front of Zara plus Zara herself. In other words, the total number of people in line is x + y + 1.

If there is one named object in line, then the total number of objects in line is (the number of objects behind the named object) + (the number of objects in front of the named object) + 1.

Step 2: Translate the given information into equations involving x and/or y.

Since we are told there are 10 people in front of Zara, we know that y = 10.

Since we are told there are 8 people behind Zara, we know that x = 8.

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

We want to determine the total number of people in line, which is equivalent to x + y + 1.

So, we can plug y = 10 and x = 8 into the expression x + y + 1:

Total number of people in line=x+y+1Total number of people in line=8+10+1Total number of people in line=19

Thus, there are 19 people in the line, including Zara.

In this question, we have just one named object, Lindsey. So, lets follow the 3-step approach to solving such questions:

Step 1: On a line, add a point representing the named object and label the diagram such that x = the number of objects behind the object and y = the number of objects in front of the object:

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

Now lets learn the technique for answering questions in which there are two named objects.

Two named objects in the line

The strategy for answering questions that have two named objects in the line is almost the same as the strategy for one named object, except we need two points for the two named objects, plus we will need a third interval.

So, for example, if we have a line of people such that person A is behind person B, we will add points for the two named people (A and B), and well have three intervals labeled x, y, and z as follows:

Lets examine some properties of this diagram:

x represents the number of people behind person A.

y represents the number of people between persons A and B.

z represents the number of people ahead of person B.

Since A and B are not included in the intervals x, y, and z, the total number of people in line is x + y + z + 2, where we add 2 so that the total includes persons A and B.

Lets use the above diagram to practice translating given information into algebraic equations.

Example: There are 20 people ahead of person A.

The people ahead of person A include the z people in front of B and the y people in between A and B. We must also account for person B. Thus, the total number of people ahead of person A is y + z + 1, and so we can write the equation y + z + 1 = 20.

Example: Person B is the 10th person from the front of the line.

If person B is the 10th person from the front, then there are 9 people ahead of person B, which means we can write z = 9.

Example: Person B is the 99th person from the back of the line.

If person B is the 99th person from the back, then there are 98 people behind person B. The people behind B include the x people behind A, the y people between A and B, and person A. In other words, x + y + 1 represents the total number of people behind person B, which means we can write the equation x + y + 1 = 98.

The 3-step technique when the question has two named objects

In general, the strategy for solving line-up problems with 2 named objects is very similar to the strategy for solving problems with 1 named object:

1. On a line, add two points representing the two named objects and label the intervals x, y, and z as follows:

2. Translate the given information into equations involving x, y and/or z.

3. Solve for the desired quantity.

Lets practice with the following mini-example:

In a line of people, Cho is ahead of Anh. Anh is the 23rd person from the front, and Cho is the 28th person from the back. If there are 15 people between Anh and Cho, how many people are in the line?

Lets follow the 3-step process.

Step 1: On a line, add two points representing the two named objects and label the intervals x, y, and z.

Since we are told that Cho is ahead of Anh, our diagram is as follows:

Step 2: Translate the given information into equations involving x, y, and/or z.

Since Anh is the 23rd person from the front, we know there are 22 people in front of Anh.

We also know that the people in front of Anh include the z people in front of Cho, the y people in between Anh and Cho, and Cho. This means we can write:

people ahead of Anh=(people between Anh and Cho)+(Cho)+(people ahead of Cho)22=y+1+zy+z=21(equation 1)

Next, since Cho is the 28th person from the back, we know there are 27 people behind Cho. We also know that the people behind Cho include the x people behind Anh, Anh, and the y people between Anh and Cho. This means we can write:

people behind Cho=x+1+y27=x+1+yx+y=26(equation 2)

Finally, if there are 15 people between Anh and Cho, we can write:

y=15(equation 3)

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

We want to determine the total number of people in the line. In other words, we want to find the value of x + y + z + 2.

First, we can take equation 2 and replace y with 15 (from equation 3) to get:

x+y=26(equation 2)x+15=26x=11

Also, we can take equation 1, and replace y with 15 (from equation 3) to get:

y+z=21(equation 1)15+z=21z=6

Since the total number of people is x + y + z + 2, we can now plug in x = 11, y = 15, and z = 6 to find this total:

Total number of people=x+y+z+2Total number of people=11+15+6+2Total number of people=34

Thus, there are 34 people in line, including Anh and Cho.

Text Solution:

Lets follow the 3-step process.

Step 1: On a line, add two points representing the two named objects, and label the intervals x, y, and z.

In making this diagram, we must be sure to place Yu behind Gil.

Step 2: Translate the given information into equations involving x, y and/or z.

Since we have a total of 66 people in line, we can write:

Total number of people=66x+y+z+2=66x+y+z=64(equation 1)

Next, since the number of people behind Gil plus the number of people ahead of Yu is 85, we can write:

(people behind Gil)+(people ahead of Yu)=85(x+1+y)+(y+1+z)=85x+2y+z+2=85x+2y+z=83(equation 2)

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

We want to determine how many people are between Yu and Gil. In other words, we want the value of y.

If we recognize that the left sides of equations 1 and 2 are almost identical except that equation 2 has the term 2y and equation 1 has the term y, well realize we can solve for y by subtracting equation 1 from equation 2.

x+2y+z=83(equation 2)(x+y+z=64)(equation 1)y=19

Thus, there are 19 people between Gil and Yu.

The correct answer is option A

Alternate Solution:

On a line, add two points representing the two named objects, and label the intervals x, y, and z.

In making this diagram, we must be sure to place Yu behind Gil.

Were told that there are 66 people in total in this line, and we are told that if we add the number of people behind Gil to the number of people in front of Yu, we get 85.

If we combine the people behind Gil and the people in front of Yu, that includes everyone in the line but double counts precisely the people between Gil and Yu. Thus, the number of people between Gil and Yu must be the difference between this overcount and the actual number of people in the line. In other words, the number of people between Gil and Yu is 85 66 = 19.

Example 75 DI Data Sufficiency

Need a Little Help?
Hi Joy, do you need a hint for this example?

If Helmut and Gretta are waiting in a line of T people, what is the value of T?

1) There are 5 people between Helmut and Gretta.

2) There are 18 people behind Helmut and 11 people ahead of Gretta.

Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) alone is not sufficient.
Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) alone is not sufficient.
BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.
Confirm your answer
Show solution Hide solution

Text Solution:

We are told that Helmut and Gretta are waiting in a line, but we arent told who is in front and who is behind. This means we must consider two possible cases.

Case A: Helmut is ahead of Gretta.

Case B: Gretta is ahead of Helmut.

If case A is true, then our diagram looks like this:

If case B is true, then our diagram looks like this:

Our goal is to determine the value of T, which is equivalent to x + y + z + 2 in both possible cases.

Now lets check out the statements.

Statement One Alone:

If there are 5 people between Helmut and Gretta, then y = 5 in both cases.

Since our goal is to determine the value of x + y + z + 2, knowing that y = 5 will not be sufficient to answer the question. We have no way of determining the values of x and z. Thus, statement one alone is not sufficient.

Eliminate answer choices A and D.

Statement Two Alone:

We must apply the information from statement 2 to both possible cases.

Case A: Helmut is ahead of Gretta.

If there are 18 people behind Helmut, then we can write:

# of people behind Helmut=18x+1+y=18x+y=17(equation 1)

If there are 11 people ahead of Gretta, then we can write:

# of people ahead of Gretta=11y+1+z=11y+z=10(equation 2)

Since our goal is to determine the value of x + y + z + 2, knowing that x + y = 17 and y + z = 10 will not be sufficient to answer the question.

Also remember that we havent even considered case B yet. So, lets do that now.

Case B: Gretta is ahead of Helmut.

If there are 18 people behind Helmut, then we can write x = 18.

If there are 11 people ahead of Gretta, then we can write z = 11.

Since our goal is to determine the value of x + y + z + 2, knowing that x = 18 and z = 11 will not be sufficient to answer the question, as we do not know the value of y.

So, as we have seen, regardless of whether case A or case B is true, statement two alone is not sufficient.

Eliminate answer choice B.

Statements One and Two Together:

From statement one, we learned that y = 5.

From statement two, we made two different conclusions based on whether case A or case B is true.

If case A is true, we learned that x + y = 17 (equation 1) and y + z = 10 (equation 2).

So, if y = 5, we can plug this into equation 1 to get x + 5 = 17, which means x = 12.

Also, if y = 5, we can plug this into equation 2 to get 5 + z = 10, which means z = 5.

Thus, if x = 12, y = 5, and z = 5, then the answer to the question is T = x + y + z + 2 = 12 + 5 + 5 + 2 = 24.

On the other hand, if case B is true, we learned that x = 18 and z = 11.

Thus, if x = 18, y = 5, and z = 11, then the answer to the question is T = x + y + z + 2 = 18 + 5 + 11 + 2 = 36.

Since we cannot answer the question with certainty, statements one and two together are not sufficient.

Correct answer: E

Text Solution:

Upon checking the answer choices, you may have been surprised to see that the question could have more than one solution. The reason for this possibility is that the question does not state whether Adam is ahead of Ben in the line, or whether Ben is ahead of Adam. Given this, we must consider both cases.

Lets start with the first possible case.

Adam is ahead of Ben in the line.

Lets follow the 3-step process.

Step 1: On a line, add two points representing the two named objects and label the intervals x, y, and z.

In this case, lets be sure to put Adam in front of Ben:

Step 2: Translate the given information into equations involving x, y and/or z.

If Adam is the 10th person from the beginning of the line, then there are 9 people ahead of Adam, which means z = 9 (equation 1).

If Ben is the 15th person from the back of the line, then there are 14 people behind Ben, which means x = 14 (equation 2).

Finally, if there are 5 people in between Adam and Ben, then y = 5 (equation 3).

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

We want to determine the total number of people in the line, which is equal to x + y + z + 2.

So, we can now plug in x = 14, y = 5 and z = 9:

Total number of people=x+y+z+2Total number of people=14+5+9+2Total number of people=30

Thus, there could be 30 people in line, which means option I is one possibility for the number of people in line.

Now lets solve the second possible case.

Ben is ahead of Adam in the line.

We will, once again, follow the 3-step process.

Step 1: On a line, add two points representing the two named objects and label the intervals x, y, and z.

This time, well put Ben in front of Adam:

Step 2: Translate the given information into equations involving x, y and/or z.

If Adam is the 10th person from the beginning of the line, then there are 9 people ahead of Adam, which means we can write:

Number of people ahead of Adam=9y+1+z=9y+z=8(equation 1)

If Ben is the 15th person from the back of the line, then there are 14 people behind Ben, which means we can write:

Number of people behind Ben=14x+1+y=14x+y=13(equation 2)

Finally, if there are 5 people in between Adam and Ben, then y = 5 (equation 3).

Step 3: Solve for the desired quantity.

We want to determine the total number of people in the line, which is equal to x + y + z + 2.

We can take equation 1 and plug in y = 5 (from equation 3) in order to solve for z:

y+z=8(equation 1)5+z=8z=3

Similarly, we can take equation 2 and plug in y = 5 (from equation 3) to solve for x:

x+y=13(equation 2)x+5=13x=8

Now that we know x = 8, y = 5 and z = 3, we can determine the total number of people in the line:

Total number of people=x+y+z+2Total number of people=8+5+3+2Total number of people=18

Thus, there could be 18 people in line, which means option III is another possibility for the total number of people in the line.

Of the three options, only I and III are possible, which means the correct answer is E.

If you answered A (I only) or C (III only), then you didnt notice that we must consider two possible configurations, Ben in front of Adam, and Adam in front of Ben.

Correct answer: E

Coordinate Geometry is Currently Tested on the GMAT
Updated on: Jul 2025

While most geometrical concepts were removed from the GMAT Focus Edition, which GMAC now refers to simply as "the GMAT" once again, Coordinate Geometry is still tested.

In addition, some basic geometry concepts, including calculating the area and perimeter of shapes like squares, triangles, and circles, are still tested on the current GMAT. However, whenever a geometry formula outside of coordinate geometry is required, it will be provided in the question stem.
